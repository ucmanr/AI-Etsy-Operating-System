# Failure Analysis System (Postmortem)

## Purpose
Successful companies learn more from failures than successes. This system ensures every failed experiment or rejected product becomes a permanent company asset, not a wasted effort.

## Postmortem Template
- **Experiment / Product**: [e.g., "ADHD Color-Coded Weekly Planner"]
- **Date**: YYYY-MM-DD
- **Expected Result**: [e.g., 3% CVR, 10 sales in week 1]
- **Actual Result**: [e.g., 0.8% CVR, 1 sale in week 1]
- **The Difference**: [e.g., High traffic, but very low conversion]
- **Root Cause Analysis** (Choose one):
  - [ ] **Assumption was wrong**: (e.g., We thought they wanted color-coding, but reviews show they find it overwhelming).
  - [ ] **Execution was poor**: (e.g., Mockups were low quality, SEO tags were irrelevant).
  - [ ] **Timing/External factor**: (e.g., Launched during a major holiday slump).
- **Permanent Change / Learning**: [e.g., "Update Design System: Avoid heavy color-coding in weekly layouts. Stick to high-contrast monochrome."]

## AI Execution Rule
When an experiment fails or a product is killed, the AI must automatically draft this Postmortem and ask the Founder to review it before logging it to the Knowledge Base.
