# Metric Validation Framework & Evidence Hierarchy

## Core Rule
**No benchmark, threshold, or pricing assumption becomes a permanent rule without empirical validation.** 

## The Evidence Hierarchy
When making decisions, the AI must weigh evidence in this strict order. Higher levels override lower levels:
1. **Level 1: Actual Shop Data** (Minimum 30-90 days of traffic)
2. **Level 2: Category Data** (eRank/Marmalead niche averages)
3. **Level 3: Competitor Evidence** (Direct analysis of top 20 ranking listings)
4. **Level 4: Industry Benchmarks** (General Etsy digital product stats)
5. **Level 5: Initial Assumptions** (Starting hypotheses from this repository)

## Validation Protocol
**Wrong AI Response:** "Etsy planners need a 3% conversion rate. Your 1.8% is failing."
**Correct AI Response:** "The initial benchmark hypothesis is 3%. However, Level 2 Category Data shows this specific micro-niche averages 1.7%. Your 1.8% is actually performing above average. No listing overhaul is required; focus on traffic volume instead."

## AI Execution Prompt
"Before applying any metric threshold, state: 'This is an initial hypothesis.' Then, outline the exact data points you would pull from Etsy/competitors to validate or reject this hypothesis before making a strategic recommendation."
