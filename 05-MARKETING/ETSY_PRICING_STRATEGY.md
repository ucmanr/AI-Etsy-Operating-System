# Etsy Pricing Strategy

## Purpose
To maximize revenue and perceived value without competing in a "race to the bottom" on price.

## Core Pricing Philosophy
- **Value-Based, Not Cost-Based**: Price based on the transformation the product provides (e.g., "saves 5 hours a week"), not the 30 minutes it took to design.
- **The "Sweet Spot"**: For digital ADHD productivity tools, the sweet spot is typically `$7 - $15` for single items, and `$15 - $25` for bundles.

## Pricing Tiers
1. **Entry Level (Lead Magnet)**: `$0 - $3`. (e.g., A single-page "Dopamine Menu" template). Goal: Capture emails or generate quick, low-friction sales to boost shop ranking.
2. **Core Product**: `$7 - $12`. (e.g., The main Daily/Weekly Planner). Goal: Primary revenue driver.
3. **Premium Bundle**: `$15 - $25`. (e.g., Daily Planner + Weekly Review + Habit Tracker + Sticker Pack). Goal: Maximize Average Order Value (AOV).

## Discount Psychology
- **Never run a permanent "50% off" sale**. It devalues the product.
- **Use "Launch Pricing"**: "Introductory price of `$7` for the first 50 buyers, then `$12`." This creates urgency.
- **Bundle Discount**: "Buy the bundle and save 30% compared to buying individually."
