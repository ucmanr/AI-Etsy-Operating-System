# Listing Copy Framework

## Purpose
To write product descriptions that convert browsers into buyers by addressing specific ADHD pain points and overcoming objections.

## Structure (The "AIDA" Adaptation for Neurodivergent Buyers)
1. **The Hook (Attention)**: Call out the specific struggle. 
   *Example*: "Tired of buying planners you abandon by week two?"
2. **The Empathy & Solution (Interest)**: Validate their experience and introduce the product as the antidote.
   *Example*: "Traditional planners are too rigid. This flexible, dopamine-friendly tracker adapts to your brain, not the other way around."
3. **The Features as Benefits (Desire)**: Bullet points explaining *why* a feature matters.
   *Example*: "✅ 'Brain Dump' Section: Get overwhelming thoughts out of your head instantly."
4. **The Friction Remover (Action)**: Clear, step-by-step instructions on how to use it, removing purchase anxiety.
   *Example*: "1. Purchase & Download. 2. Import to Goodnotes. 3. Start planning in 30 seconds."

## Strict Constraints
- Use short paragraphs (max 2-3 sentences).
- Use emojis sparingly and consistently as visual anchors (e.g., ✅, 🧠, 📌).
- Never use guilt-inducing language (e.g., "Stop being lazy"). Use empowering language (e.g., "Work with your brain's natural rhythm").
