# Accessibility & Neurodivergent Design Rules

## Purpose
Non-negotiable rules to ensure our products are genuinely usable by the ADHD/neurodivergent target audience.

## The "Zero Friction" Rules
1. **The 3-Second Rule**: A user must understand the purpose of a page within 3 seconds of looking at it.
2. **No "Wall of Text"**: Break all instructions into bullet points, checkboxes, or short, actionable phrases.
3. **Forgiving Layouts**: Include "Catch-up" or "Brain Dump" sections. Do not design rigid, hour-by-hour schedules that induce guilt if a slot is missed.
4. **Clear Affordances**: Buttons, checkboxes, and fillable areas must look obviously interactive (e.g., distinct borders, subtle shadows).
5. **Dyslexia-Friendly**: Avoid justified text alignment (use left-aligned). Ensure adequate letter-spacing.

## QA Check
Before any design is approved, it must be viewed at 50% zoom. If the hierarchy and primary action are not immediately obvious, it fails QA.
